## Part 1: Content Depth Standards (The "What")

### 1.1 Module Content Requirements (Enhanced)

**Current Standard:**
- 6-8 video lectures per module
- Basic lecture notes
- Standard assessments
- Biblical integration

**NEW Elite Standard (Per Module):**

#### A. Video Lectures (8-12 per module, 60-90 min each)
- **Theoretical Depth**: Graduate-level rigor with proofs, derivations, mathematical frameworks
- **Research Integration**: 5-10 cutting-edge papers (last 6 months) from top conferences
- **Expert Insights**: Guest lectures from recognized Christian leaders in the field
- **Production Quality**: Cinematic quality with professional graphics, animations, simulations
- **Interactive Elements**: Embedded quizzes, pause-and-reflect moments, hands-on demos

#### B. Written Materials (Per Module)
- **Comprehensive Lecture Notes**: 30-50 pages per module (not 10-20)
  - Deep theoretical explanations with mathematical rigor where appropriate
  - Worked examples showing expert problem-solving approaches
  - Connection to latest research and industry practices
  - Historical context and evolution of ideas
  - Future directions and open problems in the field

- **Research Paper Analysis**: 5-10 papers per module
  - Guided reading with annotation and discussion questions
  - Critical analysis frameworks
  - Connection to course concepts
  - Replication exercises where applicable

- **Industry Case Studies**: 3-5 per module
  - Real companies, real problems, real solutions
  - Interviews with Christian leaders who solved these problems
  - Financial data, technical specifications, actual outcomes
  - Ethical analysis from biblical perspective

- **Supplementary Readings**: Curated from top textbooks
  - MIT OpenCourseWare materials
  - Stanford CS229 lecture notes
  - Harvard Business School cases
  - Oxford theological commentaries
  - Plus unique Christian perspectives

#### C. Assessments (Per Module)
- **Problem Sets**: 15-20 problems per module
  - Mix of computational, theoretical, and applied problems
  - Difficulty range: 40% intermediate, 40% advanced, 20% research-level
  - Require 10-15 hours to complete properly
  - Auto-graded + human-reviewed components

- **Mini-Projects**: 1-2 per module
  - Build something real (code, business plan, theological analysis)
  - Deploy to actual users/communities
  - Measure real-world impact
  - Portfolio-ready deliverables

- **Research Exercises**: 1 per module
  - Replicate a result from a paper
  - Extend an existing method
  - Analyze a dataset
  - Write a mini-paper (3-5 pages)

#### D. Interactive Components
- **Live Coding Sessions**: For technical courses
- **Case Study Discussions**: Socratic method with AI facilitation
- **Peer Review Exercises**: Students critique each other's work
- **Office Hours**: With faculty and industry mentors
- **Research Seminars**: Students present findings

#### E. Spiritual Integration (Enhanced)
- **Biblical Foundations**: Not just verses, but deep theological frameworks
  - How does this topic reveal God's character?
  - What does Scripture say about this domain?
  - How does Christ's Lordship apply here?
  
- **Ethical Analysis**: Real dilemmas from the field
  - Case studies of Christian leaders facing tough decisions
  - Framework for biblical decision-making
  - Integration of wisdom literature and prophetic insight

- **Calling Discernment**: Practical application
  - How might God use this knowledge in your calling?
  - What problems in the world could you solve with this?
  - Who are you becoming through this learning?
