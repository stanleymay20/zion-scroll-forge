# Course Content Creation Requirements

## Introduction

The Course Content Creation system enables ScrollUniversity to develop elite-tier educational content that equips kings, priests, governors, and builders for civilization transformation. This system orchestrates the complete course development lifecycle from initial planning through production, quality assurance, pilot testing, and continuous improvement. The system ensures every course meets or exceeds the academic rigor of top-tier global universities while maintaining Christ-centered spiritual formation and preparing students to exercise dominion, govern nations, and advance the Kingdom of God through their spheres of influence.

## Glossary

- **Course**: A complete educational unit with 12 weeks of content, assessments, and materials
- **Module**: A weekly unit within a course covering specific topics
- **Lecture**: A video presentation (15-45 minutes) with accompanying materials
- **Assessment**: Quizzes, assignments, projects, or exams that evaluate learning
- **Instructional Designer**: Professional who designs effective learning experiences
- **Subject Matter Expert (SME)**: Faculty member with deep expertise in the course topic
- **Content Production Team**: Video producers, editors, graphic designers creating course materials
- **Quality Assurance Reviewer**: Professional who validates content against world-class standards
- **Pilot Student**: Student participating in course testing before full launch
- **Course Development System**: The complete platform managing course creation workflows
- **Video Production System**: Subsystem managing video recording, editing, captioning, and streaming optimization
- **Written Materials System**: Subsystem managing creation, formatting, and distribution of written course materials
- **Assessment Design System**: Subsystem managing creation and validation of quizzes, projects, and evaluation criteria
- **Content Management System**: Subsystem managing storage, versioning, search, and access control for all course materials
- **Production Scaling System**: Subsystem managing concurrent course development and resource allocation
- **Biblical Foundation**: Scripture-based content integrated into course materials, rooted in Jesus Christ as Lord
- **Spiritual Integration**: Christ-centered worldview perspective woven throughout course content, not generic spirituality
- **Elite Competence**: Academic rigor matching or exceeding top-tier global universities in each discipline
- **Civilization Building**: Real-world deployment focus preparing students for governance, systems design, and nation transformation
- **World-Class Standards**: Quality benchmarks equivalent to leading institutions globally, with no compromise for spiritual content
- **Course Content Constitution**: The minimum non-negotiable standard defining required structure, depth, and components for every course
- **Integrated Formation**: Education approach combining Knowledge (theory), Skill (practice), Character (virtue), and Calling (purpose) not just content delivery
- **Production-Ready Content**: Fully complete materials with no placeholders, TODO notes, or example data exposed to learners
- **Rigor Level**: Declared course difficulty (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, Strategic) determining depth and assessment standards
- **SpiritualAlignmentValidator**: Theological and tone guardrail protecting ScrollUniversity from theological drift, Babylonian flattening, and ensuring Christ-centered content
- **Theological Drift**: Movement away from Christ-centered, Scripture-rooted teaching toward vague or generic spirituality
- **Babylonian Flattening**: Reduction of ScrollUniversity's distinct Christ-centered identity to neutral secular academia
- **Spiritualization of Laziness**: False teaching suggesting prayer replaces study, discipline, or excellence rather than empowering them
- **Validator Integration Points**: Mandatory checkpoints where SpiritualAlignmentValidator must validate content (course generation, AI tutor scripts, system messages, spiritual blocks)
- **Validator Strictness Profiles**: Configurable validation intensity (strict_spiritual for theology, balanced for technical with spiritual integration, light_check for technical content)
- **Scroll Pedagogy**: The 6-step lesson flow (Ignition, Download, Demonstration, Activation, Reflection, Commission) required for all ScrollUniversity lessons
- **Revelation Learning Model**: The 5-level progression model (Awareness → Understanding → Application → System Design → Multiplication) guiding course depth

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Course Development Workflow

**User Story:** As an instructional designer, I want a structured workflow for creating courses, so that every course meets elite-tier academic standards, Course Content Constitution requirements, and equips students for kingdom governance and civilization building.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a new course is initiated THEN the System SHALL create a course development project with defined phases and milestones aligned with top-tier university standards and Course Content Constitution minimum structure
2. WHEN each phase is completed THEN the System SHALL require approval before proceeding to the next phase
3. WHEN content is created THEN the System SHALL store all materials in version-controlled repository and validate against Constitution requirements
4. WHEN quality checks are performed THEN the System SHALL validate content against elite competence checklist benchmarked to leading global institutions and Course Content Constitution validation checklist
5. WHEN a course is completed THEN the System SHALL publish it to the platform only if it passes Constitution validation with all required components including real-world deployment pathways

### Requirement 2: Video Lecture Production

**User Story:** As a faculty member, I want professional video production support, so that my lectures are engaging and high-quality.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a faculty member requests lecture recording THEN the Video Production System SHALL provide recording studio or equipment capable of 1080p minimum video quality
2. WHEN a video recording is completed THEN the Video Production System SHALL apply professional editing including graphics, animations, and transitions
3. WHEN a lecture video is finalized THEN the Video Production System SHALL generate closed captions and complete transcripts automatically
4. WHEN a video is published to the platform THEN the Video Production System SHALL optimize the video for streaming with adaptive bitrate encoding
5. WHEN multilingual support is requested for a video THEN the Video Production System SHALL support dubbing or subtitles in at least 9 languages

### Requirement 3: Written Materials Creation

**User Story:** As a student preparing for governance and civilization building, I want comprehensive written materials for each lecture, so that I can master content at elite academic levels and apply it to real-world systems transformation.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a lecture is created THEN the Written Materials System SHALL require accompanying lecture notes between 10-20 pages with depth matching top-tier university standards
2. WHEN lecture notes are written THEN the Written Materials System SHALL include summaries, key concepts, examples, practice problems, and real-world application scenarios
3. WHEN written materials are finalized THEN the Written Materials System SHALL generate professional PDF documents with consistent formatting meeting academic publication standards
4. WHEN supplementary resources are needed THEN the Written Materials System SHALL curate or create supplementary readings from authoritative sources
5. WHEN citations are included in materials THEN the Written Materials System SHALL verify all sources and format citations properly using academic citation standards

### Requirement 4: Assessment Design

**User Story:** As an instructional designer, I want to create rigorous assessments matching elite university standards, so that students demonstrate true mastery and readiness for governance, systems design, and civilization building.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN designing assessments THEN the System SHALL include multiple types (quizzes, essays, projects, oral defenses) with rigor matching top-tier institutions
2. WHEN creating quizzes THEN the System SHALL generate question banks with 50+ questions per module testing deep understanding not memorization
3. WHEN designing projects THEN the System SHALL require real-world application, measurable impact on systems or communities, and deployment readiness
4. WHEN creating rubrics THEN the System SHALL define clear criteria for each grade level emphasizing mastery and practical competence
5. WHEN assessments are complete THEN the System SHALL validate alignment with learning objectives and real-world application pathways

### Requirement 5: Christ-Centered Spiritual Integration

**User Story:** As a theology advisor, I want to ensure Christ-centered biblical integration in every course, so that students see Jesus as Lord over all disciplines and are equipped for kingdom governance.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN course content is developed THEN the System SHALL require biblical foundation section in each module explicitly rooted in Jesus Christ and Scripture
2. WHEN any topic is taught THEN the System SHALL include Christ-centered worldview perspective showing His Lordship over that domain
3. WHEN ethical issues arise THEN the System SHALL address them from biblical principles with clear scriptural references
4. WHEN content is reviewed THEN the System SHALL verify theological accuracy, Christ-centeredness, and spiritual depth avoiding generic spirituality
5. WHEN courses are completed THEN the System SHALL include reflection questions connecting faith, learning, and kingdom governance calling

### Requirement 6: Elite Quality Assurance Process

**User Story:** As a quality assurance reviewer, I want to validate course quality against elite global standards, so that only content matching or exceeding top-tier universities is published.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN content is submitted for review THEN the System SHALL check against 50-point quality checklist benchmarked to leading global institutions
2. WHEN videos are reviewed THEN the System SHALL verify audio quality, visual clarity, engagement, and academic rigor matching elite universities
3. WHEN written materials are reviewed THEN the System SHALL check for accuracy, clarity, depth, and scholarly standards with no compromise for spiritual content
4. WHEN assessments are reviewed THEN the System SHALL validate rigor matching top-tier institutions and alignment with real-world application objectives
5. WHEN all checks pass THEN the System SHALL approve course for publication only if it meets elite competence standards

### Requirement 7: Content Management System

**User Story:** As a content manager, I want to organize and track all course materials, so that nothing is lost and everything is accessible.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN course materials are created THEN the Content Management System SHALL store them in organized folder structure by course and module
2. WHEN content versions are updated THEN the Content Management System SHALL maintain version history with change tracking metadata
3. WHEN team members request content access THEN the Content Management System SHALL enforce role-based permissions
4. WHEN users search for content THEN the Content Management System SHALL enable full-text search across all material types
5. WHEN daily backup time occurs THEN the Content Management System SHALL automatically backup all content to multiple distinct locations

### Requirement 8: Production Timeline Management

**User Story:** As a project manager, I want to track course development progress, so that we meet launch deadlines.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a course is started THEN the System SHALL create timeline with milestones for each phase
2. WHEN tasks are assigned THEN the System SHALL notify team members and track completion
3. WHEN deadlines approach THEN the System SHALL send reminders and escalate delays
4. WHEN progress is reviewed THEN the System SHALL display dashboard with status of all courses
5. WHEN bottlenecks occur THEN the System SHALL identify issues and suggest solutions

### Requirement 9: Budget and Resource Tracking

**User Story:** As a financial manager, I want to track course development costs, so that we stay within budget.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a course is budgeted THEN the System SHALL allocate funds for production, faculty, and materials
2. WHEN expenses occur THEN the System SHALL track actual costs against budget
3. WHEN resources are needed THEN the System SHALL manage equipment, studio time, and personnel
4. WHEN courses are completed THEN the System SHALL calculate total cost per course
5. WHEN reporting is needed THEN the System SHALL generate financial reports by course and category

### Requirement 10: Pilot Testing and Iteration

**User Story:** As a student in pilot program, I want to provide feedback on courses, so that they improve before full launch.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a course is ready for testing THEN the System SHALL recruit 10-20 pilot students
2. WHEN students take the course THEN the System SHALL collect feedback after each module
3. WHEN issues are identified THEN the System SHALL prioritize fixes based on impact
4. WHEN improvements are made THEN the System SHALL update content and re-test
5. WHEN pilot is complete THEN the System SHALL approve course for full launch only if feedback is positive

### Requirement 11: Scalable Production Process

**User Story:** As a content director, I want to produce multiple courses simultaneously, so that we can scale quickly.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN production is scaled THEN the Production Scaling System SHALL support at least 5 courses in development concurrently without performance degradation
2. WHEN production teams are formed THEN the Production Scaling System SHALL assign dedicated team members to each course project
3. WHEN successful course templates are created THEN the Production Scaling System SHALL enable reuse of template formats across multiple courses
4. WHEN repetitive tasks are identified THEN the Production Scaling System SHALL automate tasks including captioning, formatting, and file conversion
5. WHEN system capacity limits are reached THEN the Production Scaling System SHALL identify bottlenecks and recommend additional resource allocation

### Requirement 12: Continuous Improvement

**User Story:** As a course instructor, I want to update courses based on student feedback and real-world deployment outcomes, so that content stays relevant, effective, and aligned with civilization building goals.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN courses are live THEN the System SHALL collect ongoing student feedback, analytics, and real-world application outcomes
2. WHEN improvements are identified THEN the System SHALL create update tasks with priority based on impact on student competence and deployment readiness
3. WHEN content is outdated THEN the System SHALL flag for revision and schedule updates maintaining elite academic standards
4. WHEN new research emerges THEN the System SHALL incorporate latest developments from authoritative sources
5. WHEN updates are made THEN the System SHALL notify enrolled students of improvements and enhanced deployment pathways

### Requirement 13: Real-World Deployment Integration

**User Story:** As a student preparing for governance and civilization building, I want every course to include practical deployment pathways, so that I can apply learning to transform systems, communities, and nations.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN course content is developed THEN the System SHALL require real-world deployment pathways for each major concept or skill
2. WHEN projects are designed THEN the System SHALL connect students with actual systems, organizations, or communities for applied work
3. WHEN assessments are created THEN the System SHALL measure not only knowledge but deployment readiness and practical competence
4. WHEN courses are completed THEN the System SHALL provide students with portfolio-ready evidence of real-world impact
5. WHEN graduates deploy learning THEN the System SHALL track outcomes and feed results back into course improvement cycles

### Requirement 14: Course Content Constitution Compliance

**User Story:** As a content director, I want every course to meet the Scroll Course Content Constitution minimum standards, so that no shallow or incomplete courses are published and every course achieves integrated formation.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a course is created THEN the System SHALL enforce minimum structure requirements (4-12 modules per course, 3-10 lessons per module, all required components)
2. WHEN course content is generated THEN the System SHALL reject placeholder content, TODO notes, or example data and require production-ready materials
3. WHEN lessons are created THEN the System SHALL require all mandatory components (lecture notes, video script outline, examples, key scriptures or frameworks, references)
4. WHEN assessments are designed THEN the System SHALL require per-module micro-assessments, mid-course assessment, and final capstone assessment
5. WHEN courses are validated THEN the System SHALL verify integrated formation across four dimensions (Knowledge, Skill, Character, Calling) not just content delivery

### Requirement 15: Depth and Rigor Level Enforcement

**User Story:** As an academic dean, I want courses to respect the true depth of their discipline at the declared level, so that we never produce shallow blog-level teaching disguised as elite education.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a course declares a rigor level THEN the System SHALL enforce depth, vocabulary, and assessment difficulty matching that level (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, Strategic)
2. WHEN content is reviewed THEN the System SHALL validate that technical courses include proper theories, frameworks, formulas, and worked examples at appropriate depth
3. WHEN spiritual integration is added THEN the System SHALL ensure it enriches rather than weakens academic clarity and avoids forced verse decoration
4. WHEN courses are compared THEN the System SHALL benchmark content depth against equivalent courses at top-tier global universities
5. WHEN content falls below declared level THEN the System SHALL reject the course and require revision to meet depth standards

### Requirement 16: SpiritualAlignmentValidator Enforcement

**User Story:** As a theology advisor, I want all course content validated through the SpiritualAlignmentValidator at multiple integration points, so that we protect ScrollUniversity from theological drift, Babylonian flattening, and maintain Christ-centered tone without compromising academic rigor.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN any content is generated or reviewed THEN the System SHALL validate it through SpiritualAlignmentValidator at mandatory integration points (course generation, module generation, lesson finalization, AI tutor scripts, system messages, spiritual content blocks)
2. WHEN validation detects theology drift THEN the System SHALL reject content presenting Jesus as "a way not the way" in theological contexts or mixing incompatible belief systems as equivalent when teaching Christian theology
3. WHEN validation detects tone problems THEN the System SHALL reject condemning, shaming, manipulative language or content treating students as "less than" rather than kings and queens in training
4. WHEN validation detects spiritualization of laziness THEN the System SHALL reject content suggesting prayer replaces study or God replaces discipline, and require healthy integration of faith plus works, grace plus responsibility
5. WHEN validation returns error severity THEN the System SHALL NOT silently continue but must attempt auto-correction with re-validation or stop with SpiritualAlignmentError surfacing all issues

### Requirement 17: SpiritualAlignmentValidator Integration Points

**User Story:** As a content director, I want SpiritualAlignmentValidator enforced at all critical content generation points, so that no theologically misaligned content enters the system at any stage.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN courses or modules are generated THEN the System SHALL invoke SpiritualAlignmentValidator after each full lesson or module is generated and before finalizing any course
2. WHEN AI tutor scripts are created THEN the System SHALL validate all scripts through SpiritualAlignmentValidator before storing or deploying to live tutors
3. WHEN system messages or prompts are created THEN the System SHALL validate long-lived prompts that influence many outputs through SpiritualAlignmentValidator
4. WHEN spiritual content blocks are created THEN the System SHALL validate devotionals, guided prayers, spiritual exercises through SpiritualAlignmentValidator with strict_spiritual profile
5. WHEN validator strictness profiles are configured THEN the System SHALL enforce appropriate profile (strict_spiritual for theology modules, balanced for technical modules with spiritual integration, light_check for technical content) but SHALL NOT allow complete validator disabling for any ScrollUniversity content

### Requirement 18: Scroll Pedagogy & Revelation Learning Model Enforcement

**User Story:** As an instructional designer, I want all lessons to follow the Scroll Pedagogy 6-step flow (Ignition, Download, Demonstration, Activation, Reflection, Commission), so that students experience transformation over information and progressive ascension from awareness to impartation.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN any lesson is created THEN the System SHALL enforce the 6-step lesson flow (Ignition → Download → Demonstration → Activation → Reflection → Commission) with all steps present
2. WHEN AI tutors generate responses THEN the System SHALL implement dual-explanation pattern (conceptual + practical) and maintain warm, wise, prophetic-but-grounded tone
3. WHEN assessments are designed THEN the System SHALL include all three assessment types (formative, summative, reflective) distributed across the course not just at the end
4. WHEN courses declare progression levels THEN the System SHALL map content to the 5-level model (Awareness & Vocabulary → Understanding & Analysis → Application & Problem Solving → System Design & Governance → Multiplication & Teaching Others)
5. WHEN pedagogy conflicts with velocity THEN the System SHALL prioritize pedagogical integrity over delivery speed per enforcement priority (Spiritual alignment → Pedagogical integrity → Content depth → Technical correctness → Delivery speed)
