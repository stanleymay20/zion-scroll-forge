# COURSE 001: Sacred AI & Machine Learning Engineering
**"In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God" - John 1:1**

## Course Information

**Course Code**: SCROLLAI-101
**Credits**: 4
**Duration**: 12 Weeks
**Level**: Undergraduate (Advanced)
**Prerequisites**: Calculus II, Linear Algebra, Programming (Python)
**Instructor**: Dr. Michael Chen, PhD (MIT), 15 years AI research
**AI Dean**: ScrollDean Sophia (AI Engineering Specialist)

## Course Description

An intensive exploration of artificial intelligence and machine learning from both technical and theological perspectives. Students will master the mathematics, algorithms, and implementation of modern AI systems while grappling with profound questions about intelligence, consciousness, creativity, and the image of God. This course combines the rigor of MIT's AI curriculum with deep theological reflection on technology's role in God's kingdom.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Technical Mastery**
   - Implement neural networks and deep learning algorithms from scratch
   - Train and deploy production-quality AI systems
   - Understand and apply transformer architectures (GPT, BERT)
   - Optimize models for performance and efficiency
   - Debug and troubleshoot AI systems

2. **Theoretical Understanding**
   - Explain mathematical foundations (linear algebra, calculus, probability)
   - Understand learning theory and optimization
   - Analyze algorithmic complexity and efficiency
   - Evaluate AI capabilities and limitations
   - Engage with cutting-edge research

3. **Theological Integration**
   - Articulate biblical framework for AI development
   - Address questions of consciousness and the image of God
   - Apply Christian ethics to AI applications
   - Discern appropriate and inappropriate uses of AI
   - Develop theology of technology and creativity

4. **Practical Application**
   - Build AI systems for kingdom purposes
   - Deploy models to production environments
   - Solve real-world problems with AI
   - Collaborate on AI projects
   - Communicate technical concepts clearly

## Course Structure

### Module 1: Foundations of AI (Week 1)
- History from Turing to GPT-4
- Mathematical foundations
- Biblical perspective on intelligence
- Python and ML libraries setup

### Module 2: Machine Learning Fundamentals (Week 2)
- Supervised vs unsupervised learning
- Linear regression from scratch
- Logistic regression and classification
- Model evaluation and validation

### Module 3: Neural Networks (Week 3)
- Perceptrons and multilayer networks
- Backpropagation algorithm
- Activation functions
- Gradient descent optimization

### Module 4: Deep Learning (Week 4)
- Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)
- Image classification and computer vision
- Transfer learning
- Model architectures (ResNet, VGG, etc.)

### Module 5: Natural Language Processing (Week 5)
- Text preprocessing and embeddings
- Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs, LSTMs)
- Sequence-to-sequence models
- Attention mechanisms

### Module 6: Transformers & Large Language Models (Week 6)
- Transformer architecture deep dive
- BERT, GPT, T5 models
- Fine-tuning pre-trained models
- Prompt engineering

### Module 7: Advanced Topics (Week 7)
- Generative models (VAEs, GANs, Diffusion)
- Reinforcement learning
- Multi-modal AI (CLIP, DALL-E)
- AI safety and alignment

### Module 8: Theological Integration (Week 8)
- Can machines be conscious?
- AI and the image of God
- Ethics of AI development
- Stewardship of technology
- Eschatological implications

### Module 9: AI for Kingdom Purposes (Week 9)
- Bible translation assistance
- Discipleship tools
- Evangelism support
- Church technology
- Ministry applications

### Module 10: Production AI Systems (Week 10)
- Model deployment and serving
- Scaling and optimization
- Monitoring and maintenance
- Security and privacy
- Cost optimization

### Module 11: Research & Innovation (Week 11)
- Reading research papers
- Experimental design
- Reproducing results
- Contributing to open source
- Publishing research

### Module 12: Capstone Project (Week 12)
- Build production AI system
- Solve real-world problem
- Deploy to users
- Measure impact
- Present and defend
